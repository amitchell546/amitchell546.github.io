1. Tennessee

Visually and Functionally, the best website of these five is the Tennessee
State website. With very clearly labelled links to the different subsites and
an informative calender for public events and links to social media, this website
makes it easy for a citizen to connect and get involved with their government,
without it being tedious. The only possible complaint one could have for this
website is that it is themed too casually and lacks professionality

2. Montana

The Montana State website is a close second behind Tennessee. It improves upon
the complaint I had with the Tennessee site that lacked professionality. This
website certainly looks more professional but could use a bit more style, some of
the links don't stand out from the whitebar at the top and could make navigating
more difficult

3. West Virginia

This State's Website is the last of the five websites where I find little to
The layout of this homepage certainly makes everything available to you, albeit
in a confusing way. It took me a few minutes of using the site to realize that
more links were made available to you if you used the arrows on the left and
righthand sides of the screen. Visually, my only complaint is how sleek and
straight the navigation bar is at the top and how curvy and rugged the footer is
at the bottom of the screen, they conflict with each other and I find it slightly
annoying.

4. South Dakota

The South Dakota website isn't terrible however there are a few things visually,
and functionally that I could imagine make it difficult to use. There are very few
links to the subpages from the front page and makes it so that in order to access
additional information one would have to go through and inevitable get lost in all
of the menus the designer used in trying to keep the front page un-cluttered.
Also, on the bottom of the site, the footer casts a shadow on the image but on the
top of the page, which uses very similar design, no shadow is cast and this visually
disrupts the site

5. Louisiana

Louisiana's website is, by a significant margin, the worst website of the five.
The website uses incredibly dated and unoriginal design and looks like it hasn't
updated in years, evidence given by the fact the social media icons for the state's
youtube, facebook and twitter were all changed years ago. The use of very small font
and a lack of clear labelling for the information your looking at makes the front page
alone a nightmare.
